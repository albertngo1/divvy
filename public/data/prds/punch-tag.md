## Overview
Punch Tag is a phone app + printable QR label system for small fire-protection service companies who inspect extinguishers, exit signs, and emergency lights for their building clients. It replaces the physical monthly punch-tag with a scannable asset that carries its full inspection history.

## Problem
NFPA 10 requires monthly visual inspections of every portable extinguisher, recorded on a paper tag hung on the unit. A tech walks a 200-unit warehouse punching tags by hand; the record lives only on that tag, which fades, falls off, or gets tampered with. When a fire marshal audits, proving "we actually checked" is a scavenger hunt. Building owners have no live view of compliance across sites.

## How it works
Each asset gets a durable QR sticker generated by the app (using a real QR truetype-style renderer for crisp cheap printing). The tech scans it, sees the unit's location and last inspection, taps a checklist (pin/seal intact, gauge in green, no corrosion, access clear), optionally snaps a photo, and moves on — geolocation and timestamp captured automatically. A missed unit shows up as overdue. Owners get a per-site compliance dashboard and an exportable audit report. Anomalies (low gauge, blocked access) create a service ticket.

## Technical approach
Stack: React Native (Expo) client, offline-first with SQLite + sync to a Postgres/Supabase backend. QR labels encode a short asset UUID; the checklist schema is a versioned JSON so different jurisdictions' requirements can differ. Scans record GPS + device time + optional photo (stored in object storage, hashed). Compliance status is a derived view: `last_inspection_date` vs. required cadence per asset type. Reports render server-side to PDF. The hard part isn't tech — it's the offline sync and conflict resolution when three techs scan the same site with no signal, plus getting the checklist to exactly match NFPA cadence rules (monthly visual vs. annual maintenance vs. 6-year teardown).

## v1 scope
- Create assets, print QR labels
- Scan → monthly visual checklist with photo
- Overdue list per site
- One-tap PDF audit report

## Out of scope
- Annual/6-year maintenance workflows
- Invoicing and dispatch
- Multi-company / white-label

## Risks & unknowns
- Stickers must survive grease, UV, and abuse — durability testing needed.
- Incumbents (e.g. established inspection apps) exist; wedge is dead-simple pricing for 1–5 tech shops.
- Fire marshals in some jurisdictions may still demand a physical tag alongside digital.

## Done means
A tech inspects a 20-unit floor entirely by scanning, then generates a PDF report listing every unit, its status, timestamp, and photo — and a second device syncs those scans after being offline the whole walkthrough.
