## Overview
A desktop menubar app that borrows OpenTelemetry's distributed-tracing model and points it at *you*. For quantified-self tinkerers and engineers who profile everything except their own routines. Riffs on the trending otel-demo repo: take the serious observability stack and make it a personal toy.

## Problem
You *feel* your mornings are slow but can't see where the time goes. Time-trackers give you flat pie charts — durations, not structure. They can't show nesting ("shower" contains "argue with water temperature") or the critical path (which spans, if shortened, actually make you leave earlier vs. which run in parallel and don't matter).

## How it works
You define reusable span types (Shower, Coffee, Commute, Pack Bag). A menubar dropdown lets you start/stop spans; overlapping and nested spans are allowed, exactly like tracing. Over a day this produces a trace tree. The app renders a genuine flamegraph — width = duration, depth = nesting — and, crucially, computes the **critical path**: the longest dependency chain from wake to out-the-door, so it can tell you the coffee that runs *during* the shower is free, but the 11-minute key-hunt is pure latency. A weekly view overlays traces to show your p50/p95 morning and flags the span with the worst variance.

## Technical approach
Stack: a small Tauri or Electron menubar app (Rust/TS). Data model is literally OTel spans: {trace_id (per day), span_id, parent_span_id, name, start, end}, stored in SQLite. Reuse the OpenTelemetry semantic model but skip the collector — write spans locally. Flamegraph via a compact d3/Canvas renderer. Critical path = build the span DAG (a span depends on spans that must finish before it starts, inferred from declared sequencing rules per span type), then longest-weighted-path via topological sort. Auto-instrumentation stretch goal: derive spans from macOS app-focus events, calendar, and coarse location transitions so you tap less. The genuinely hard part is inferring true dependencies (what's actually blocking vs. incidentally sequential) — v1 punts by letting you mark spans as parallelizable.

## v1 scope
- Manual start/stop spans from the menubar, nesting allowed.
- SQLite storage, one-day flamegraph render.
- Critical-path highlight assuming strict wall-clock sequencing.

## Out of scope
- Auto-instrumentation from sensors/calendar.
- Cross-day p95 overlays and variance flags (v2).
- Any cloud sync or sharing.

## Risks & unknowns
- Tap friction: if logging is annoying, no data. Big buttons, keyboard shortcuts, and "resume last span" mitigate.
- Critical-path only means something once dependencies are modeled; strict-sequence v1 may over-count.
- Novelty could wear off — the weekly insight is what earns retention.

## Done means
After a morning of tapping spans, the app shows a flamegraph where nested/overlapping spans render correctly and the critical-path chain is highlighted, and it correctly reports that a span I ran fully in parallel contributed zero to my total wake-to-door time.
