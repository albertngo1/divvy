## Overview
Tell is a 3-6 player party game where the clip on the TV is a decoy. Each phone secretly assigns you a Mark (another player) and a prop bet on how they'll react to what's playing. You spend the clip covertly reading one face while keeping your own unreadable — because someone is betting on you too. For groups who enjoy watching each other more than the screen.

## Problem
Watching stuff together is passive: everyone stares at the TV, phones half-out, barely present with each other. The best part of group viewing has always been the reactions in the room — and that part never becomes the game.

## How it works
The host TV plays one 60-90s clip (v1: a single preset — a spicy-wing challenge, a cringe fail comp, whatever reliably gets reactions).

Before it plays, each phone privately shows: your **Mark** (a random other player, secret) and a **prop** about them, e.g. "Will [Mark] laugh OR look away in the first 30s? — OVER / UNDER" (or a small menu: winces / laughs / says something / covers eyes). You wager chips.

During the clip, two things happen on every phone simultaneously and privately. (1) You watch your Mark and cannot change your bet. (2) You log your OWN reactions with big private buttons (Laughed / Winced / Looked away) — honor system, and the room is watching you. Knowing someone may be betting on you, you're incentivized to poker-face and bust their read — but overplaying a fake reaction can throw them off too.

After the clip: reveal. Each player's self-logged reactions appear on the TV; each bettor's Mark and prop resolve; chips pay out. The comedy is the reveal that three people were all secretly staring at the one person trying to look bored.

Each phone privately shows: your Mark, your prop, your bet, your own reaction log. The shared TV shows: the clip, "bets locked," and the final reveal/payout grid.

## Technical approach
Host tab + phone PWAs + authoritative WS server (PartyKit / Cloudflare Durable Object, or Socket.IO over Tailscale Serve). One Durable Object per room.

Data model: `Room { clipId, phase }`, `Player { id, mark: playerId, prop, bet, reactionLog:{laughed,winced,lookedAway} }`. The server assigns Marks via a derangement (nobody marks themselves), holds bets secretly, and syncs a shared clip-start timestamp to all clients and the TV so reaction windows line up.

Sync strategy: the one genuinely hard part is playback-clock sync — every phone needs a shared t=0 so a "first 30 seconds" prop is fair. Broadcast a server timestamp + countdown, tolerate ~200ms skew, and anchor the clip on the TV as ground truth while phones just run a synced timer (they never stream the video). Reaction logs are private writes; bets are write-once-locked.

## v1 scope
- 3 players, one preset clip, one prop bet each.
- Random Mark assignment (derangement).
- Private reaction log with 3 buttons.
- One reveal + payout. No accounts, one round.

## Out of scope
- Clip library / upload / streaming sync of arbitrary video.
- Objective reaction detection (camera/CV) — self-report only in v1.
- Multi-round bankroll, more than ~6 players.

## Risks & unknowns
- Self-reported reactions can be gamed; social pressure may or may not hold honesty — playtest whether cheating kills it.
- 3 players is a thin Mark pool (you can often guess who's on you); it scales better at 5-6.
- Resolving "looked away" objectively without CV.

## Done means
Three phones join, each is secretly and distinctly assigned a Mark plus a prop bet, a synced clip plays on the TV while every phone privately logs its own reactions, and the reveal correctly pays out each bet against the Marks' self-logs — with no player seeing another's Mark or bet before reveal.
