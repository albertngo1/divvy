## Overview

A cooperative "silent monastery" party game. One player is the Monk who knows a hidden target answer; the group are Pilgrims who must reach it. All communication happens via emoji + private typing; any voice above a whisper on ANY phone's mic breaks the vow and fails the round. Combines the constrained-communication delight of Hanabi/Just One with real-world quiet as the enforced discipline.

## Problem

Every party-game night has one "let's calm down" round after a loud game — but the calm rounds are always turn-based prompt→vote games (Fibbage, Blank Slate). Nothing in the concurrent-room space actually *uses* silence as a mechanic. And most cooperative deduction (Hanabi, Just One) requires writing on paper or awkwardly hiding hands. Per-phone architecture lets you enforce silence with mic amplitude, and enforce "no peeking" via each screen being private.

## How it works

Room code join, everyone opens on their phone, grants mic permission at the join tap. Monk gets a category (e.g. "1993 movies", "types of pasta") and a hidden target. Monk composes an emoji sequence + timing to hint at the target — that's their only expressive channel. Pilgrims see the emoji stream in real time on their phones, then privately type guesses; Monk sees an anonymous stream of guesses appear, adjusts hints. Any phone's mic averaging above ~55dB for >2 seconds triggers a "vow broken" event: shared timer freezes, all phones vibrate, round ends in failure. Success = one Pilgrim types the exact target within a soft cap (~90s). Scoring by rounds cleared.

## Technical approach

PartyKit or Socket.IO homelab server. Room state = `{monk_id, target_word, hint_stream, guess_stream, vow_status}`. Mic monitoring runs client-side via `getUserMedia` + Web Audio API's `AnalyserNode` computing RMS every 100ms; when threshold crossed, phone emits a `vow_broken` event that server broadcasts to all. Emoji palette is fixed (~60 chosen emojis) so no keyboard needed. Categories generated by Haiku with `{count: 100, category: <topic>}` and cached. Optional: LLM-scored partial credit for near-guesses ("carbonara" when target is "cacio e pepe").

## v1 scope

1 category, 1 monk, 5-min game with best-of-3 rounds. Fixed emoji palette (60 emojis). Single mic threshold. Score = round wins. Only whisper/silent penalty logic, no partial credit. No accounts. Room code 4-letter. Web only (no PWA). Runs entirely on homelab Socket.IO.

## Out of scope

LLM guess-scoring, partial credit, multi-monk mode, adjustable difficulty, cross-round rotation of monk, tournament mode, custom category creation, spectator mode, replay.

## Risks & unknowns

Mic amplitude thresholds may vary wildly by phone hardware and ambient room noise — need per-room calibration or adaptive baseline (measure room noise floor during 5s "join" window, set threshold at floor+15dB). iOS mic permission is a hurdle; must be requested at gesture time (join tap). Category quality is the make-or-break: if targets are too obscure or too niche, no one guesses; too easy = boring. The emoji-only communication may frustrate players used to speaking freely — needs a physical marker (fingers-on-lips gesture, small sign) to reinforce the vibe.

## Done means

4 friends open the room code URL on their phones, grant mic permission, one becomes Monk, a category loads, and the group plays best-of-3 rounds successfully — with at least one round failing because someone laughed and broke the vow. If that failure moment is fun rather than annoying, v1 shipped.
