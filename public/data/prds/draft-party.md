## Overview
Draft is a 15-minute deckbuilder squeezed into party-game shape. Each phone holds a private 5-word hand. A shared prompt appears on the host screen ("the worst thing to hear from a dentist"); everyone plays one card face-down; reveal simultaneously; the group votes a winner; the loser trades one card from their hand into the winner's deck. Card values drift with the group's taste over 8 rounds — a card that flopped early can become a wrecking ball once the group figures out what the winning-est cards look like.

## Problem
Deckbuilders (Dominion, Slay the Spire, Magic drafts) are *deeply* fun but take an hour and demand attention. Party card games (Cards Against Humanity, Apples to Apples) reset the deck every round and have no arc — you never *build* anything. Nobody has built a party game where your hand evolves based on the group's judgments over the course of one sitting, and where losing a round makes your opponent's deck literally stronger.

## How it works
4-8 phones join a room. Each player is dealt a private 5-word hand from a shared word bank. Each round: a prompt appears on the host screen, everyone selects one card face-down, cards flip simultaneously, the group votes on a winner (majority, ties re-vote). Losers each pick one card from their own hand to send to the winner. The winner now has a 6-card hand and can discard down to 5 at their choice — meaning strong hands compound. Runs 8 rounds; final score is the count of rounds you won.

## Technical approach
Server-authoritative state machine per room: `waiting → prompting → playing → revealing → voting → trading → nextRound`. Each phone's private hand lives only on the server + the owning phone's socket; other players never receive it. WebSocket broadcasts are scoped: prompt + reveal + vote-tally are room-wide, hand updates + trade-target selection are unicast. Word bank is a static JSON of ~400 words v1. Prompts optionally generated by Claude Haiku on room-create for freshness, cached per room; fallback to a hand-authored bank of 60.

## v1 scope
- 8 fixed rounds
- Static ~400-word bank, hand-authored 60-prompt bank (no LLM in v1)
- Majority vote with random tiebreak; no vote-weight or anti-collusion
- Trade = loser picks card to give up (no auction, no counter-offer)

## Out of scope
- LLM-generated prompts (deferred; hooks exist)
- Card rarities, upgrade/burn mechanics, deck size beyond 5
- Persistent decks across sessions, custom word packs

## Risks & unknowns
- Word bank quality — 400 words is small; may feel repetitive on repeat play
- Group-vote judging is inherently political and can turn mean; may need a "no-vote-for-self" rule
- 8 rounds may be too long or too short — needs playtesting on the trade-compounding curve

## Done means
Six phones in a room complete an 8-round session where at least one player finishes with a fully-traded-in deck (all 5 original cards replaced), and the final scoreboard reflects vote wins accurately — verified against a scripted playthrough.
