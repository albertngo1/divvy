## Overview

One player is the Anchor of a live news broadcast. Every other player is a Correspondent, and each of their phones shows a random "story card" — a headline plus 3-5 keyword prompts (e.g. "SQUIRREL MAYOR / bribery, acorns, city hall, resignation"). The Anchor's phone shows a running teleprompter of all the correspondents' story titles and a 60-second countdown. The Anchor's job: stitch every correspondent's story into a single coherent 60-second newscast, live, cutting to each correspondent to riff on their keywords when introduced. Per-phone is load-bearing: only that correspondent can see their story card, so the Anchor is genuinely improvising off names and titles alone.

## Problem

The best party games (Cards Against Humanity, JackBox's Quiplash) are about collective improvisation but rarely give someone a hard structural role — a leader who has to weave everyone else's contributions into a whole. Improv-troupe "Harold" games do this in person, but there's no browser party game version. LLM-generated story cards make this trivially runnable at party scale, and the phone-as-personal-teleprompter is the key affordance — each correspondent gets *just* their card, the Anchor gets *just* the titles.

## How it works

Room code join, 4-8 players. One player is chosen as Anchor (rotates round to round). Each other player's phone shows a story card generated by Haiku: a punchy fake headline plus 3-5 keyword hints they should work in. The Anchor's phone shows a producer's view: a list of correspondents' names and headlines, in a suggested airing order, plus a big 60-second countdown timer and a "cut to next correspondent" button. Anchor speaks aloud, introduces each correspondent by name ("For more on the squirrel mayor scandal, we go to Marcus..."), correspondent speaks aloud for ~10 seconds riffing off their keywords, Anchor cuts back and stitches to the next story. After the round, group votes on best correspondent moment and Anchor gets a "was it coherent?" thumbs-up/down.

## Technical approach

Haiku for story-card generation with a batch prompt per round: `Generate <N> absurd news headlines. For each, output {headline, 3-5 keyword hints}. Themes should be varied (politics, science, local, entertainment, weird).` Prompt caching on the meta-prompt structure across rounds. WebSocket room state = `{round, anchor_id, correspondents: [{id, headline, keywords, delivered}], votes}`. Each phone gets ONLY its own story card in the payload; Anchor gets only headlines + names, not keywords (so the improv is genuine). Voice is entirely IRL — the phones do not stream audio.

## v1 scope

4-6 players, 3 rounds (Anchor rotates), Haiku-generated story cards (5-6 per round), 60-second broadcast timer, Anchor "cut to correspondent" button, post-round thumbs-up on Anchor + best-correspondent vote. No scoring across rounds beyond a running tally of Anchor thumbs-ups and correspondent-of-the-round wins.

## Out of scope

Voice recording / playback, live audience reactions (laugh track), Anchor teleprompter suggestions from the LLM, custom story themes, tournaments, spectator mode, video, scored "coherence" from an LLM listener, ad breaks between stories.

## Risks & unknowns

Story-card quality is the entire game — if Haiku's headlines are bland, nobody has anything to riff on; if they're too weird, correspondents freeze up. Batch prompting with a strong style guide + a few dozen human-curated examples in the prompt as few-shot should get us there. Anchor role is hard — introverts will struggle. May need an "easy mode" where the LLM suggests transition sentences on the Anchor's screen. 60 seconds may be too tight for 5 stories = 12s each; may need to be 90s. Rotating Anchor is essential — the game becomes a spotlight-hog nightmare if it doesn't.

## Done means

Four friends around a coffee table, one holding their phone like Walter Cronkite, gravely announcing "for more on the acorn embezzlement scandal, we go now to Priya." Priya reads three of her keywords and improvises the rest and everyone loses it. If someone asks to be Anchor next, v1 shipped.
