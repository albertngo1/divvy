## Overview
Lease is a self-hosted ambient artifact for homelabbers. Instead of NetBox's manual source-of-truth spreadsheet, Lease *watches* your network passively and renders a single evolving SVG poster: an apartment building where every device that ever touched your LAN is a tenant occupying a unit, annotated with move-in date, tenure, and a one-line bio. Over a year it becomes a churn ledger of your digital household.

## Problem
You have no idea what's actually on your network over time. Guests' phones, a smart bulb you returned, the roommate's laptop, a Chromecast that died — they appear and disappear with zero record. Inventory tools (NetBox) demand you type it all in; nobody does. And nothing turns the ebb and flow into something you'd *hang on a wall*.

## How it works
A nightly job discovers presence (passive ARP sniffing + periodic arp-scan + mDNS/SSDP/DHCP), resolves each device's vendor from its OUI, and diffs against yesterday. New MAC → "moved in" (assigned a unit). Absent for N days → "moved out" (unit greys, gains a vacancy plaque). Long residents get bigger, warmer units. An optional local LLM writes a deadpan tenant bio ("Unit 3B — a Espressif smart plug, quiet, keeps to itself, pays rent every 90 seconds"). Output: one self-updating `poster.svg` + `history.jsonl`.

## Technical approach
Python. Discovery via scapy (passive) + `arp-scan` + zeroconf (mDNS) + parsing `dnsmasq.leases`. Identity is the hard part: iOS/Android randomize MACs per-SSID, so a phone would spawn a new "tenant" constantly. Cluster devices into stable identities using a Fingerbank-style DHCP fingerprint (option-55 param list), mDNS hostname, and TTL/vendor signature — assign a persistent tenant id even as the MAC rotates. Storage: SQLite (`devices`, `sightings`, `tenancy_spans`). Rendering: server-side SVG (Jinja + templated units on a grid), regenerated by cron; serve via any static host or Tailscale. LLM bios optional via local Ollama, cached so text is stable.

## v1 scope
- arp-scan + mDNS nightly sweep, OUI vendor lookup
- SQLite move-in/move-out tracking with an N-day vacancy rule
- Static SVG poster of occupied/vacant units with tenure sizing
- JSONL export of the full history

## Out of scope
- MAC-randomization clustering (v1 treats each MAC as a tenant; note the caveat)
- LLM bios (nice-to-have behind a flag)
- Alerting / security posture / rogue-device detection

## Risks & unknowns
- Passive sniffing needs the host on the right VLAN/promiscuous mode; may miss segmented IoT nets
- MAC randomization makes phone tenants noisy until clustering lands
- "Absent" is ambiguous — a device off for a week isn't gone; tune the vacancy threshold

## Done means
After three days running on a real home network, `poster.svg` shows at least one "moved in" and one "moved out" tenant with correct dates drawn from actual observed presence, and `history.jsonl` records the sighting spans that produced them.
