## Problem
Budgeting apps are spreadsheets with anxiety bolted on. They tell you the truth but they make you feel nothing, so the forgotten $14 subscription and the looming card due-date never acquire any *dread* until they hurt you.

## What it is
A top-down survival-horror mini-game built from your actual recurring charges. Each bill is a "generator" placed on a map, sized by amount and lit by urgency (due-date proximity). You have the month to run around repairing (paying/allocating toward) generators from your income pool. A slow, inevitable Killer — the 1st of the month — patrols the map; anything unrepaired when it arrives gets hooked and screams (late fee, overdraft, a sub you meant to cancel auto-renewing in your face). It reframes the dry recurring-charge list as a tense round you can actually win, and the emotional feedback is the product.

## v1 (humiliatingly small)
Upload a CSV of recurring charges (or type five in). Each becomes a labeled circle on a plain canvas. Drag your "income" token onto a circle to repair it (it turns green). A timer bar crosses the screen once; whatever's still red at the end flashes and shows the fee. No pathfinding, no sprites — one round, one map.

## Done means
You paste in your real recurring bills, play one 60-second round, lose one generator to the deadline, and genuinely wince at the red circle you forgot — then immediately want to cancel it.
