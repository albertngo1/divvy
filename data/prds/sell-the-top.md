## Problem
'Memoirs of Extraordinary Popular Delusions' (1852) is a catalog of bubbles humans never learn from. Every 'guess the peak' game pits you against a chart — none pit you against the *crowd*, which is the whole point of the book.

## What it is
A once-a-day browser game. You watch an unlabeled price line tick forward day-by-day for a real historical bubble and hit SELL once. Your score isn't just profit — it's your rank *against how the real crowd behaved*: 'You sold 3 days past the tulip peak — along with 94% of Haarlem.' Reveal the bubble's identity and a Mackay quote after you play. Shareable spoiler-free result grid.

## v1 (humiliatingly small)
One hardcoded dataset (Dutch tulip index, ~40 daily points). Static page: animate the line, one SELL button, compute % of theoretical max captured, show a canned 'crowd behaved like this' line and the reveal. No accounts, no real daily rotation yet — 'daily' is faked with one puzzle.

## Done means
I can play one bubble start-to-finish, get a score framed as 'you vs. the crowd,' see the Mackay reveal, and copy a shareable result to clipboard.
